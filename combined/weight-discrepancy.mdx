## Product Info

### Weight Terminology

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Entered Weight | Weight entered by the seller while processing the order. Minimum default: 0.5 kg. |
| Dead Weight | Physical weight of the package as measured on a scale. |
| Volumetric Weight | Calculated as L × B × H / 5000. |
| Applicable Weight | The higher of Dead Weight or Volumetric Weight — used by courier for freight charges. |
| Chargeable Weight | The weight the courier uses to calculate the final charge. |

### What is Weight Discrepancy?

Weight discrepancy arises when there is a difference between the weight entered by the seller and the actual weight recorded by the courier company. During this period, Shiprocket holds the additional amount on-hold. Sellers are notified via a pop-up and email to take action.

### Weight Discrepancy Statuses

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| New Discrepancy | Weight discrepancy generated — differs from applied weight |
| Dispute Raised | Seller has rejected the courier's claim; Shiprocket raises a dispute |
| Discrepancy Accepted | Seller accepted the new weight applied by courier |
| Dispute Rejected by Courier | Courier rejected the dispute raised by the seller |
| Dispute Accepted by Courier | Courier accepted the dispute raised by the seller |
| Auto Accept | Seller did not take action within 7 days; weight is auto-accepted |
| SR Credit | On-hold amount released when courier accepts weight dispute or SR reduces applied weight |

### Auto-Accept Rule

If a seller does not dispute or accept a weight discrepancy within **7 days**, it is auto-accepted. Shiprocket sends 3 reminder emails — on the same day, on Day 4, and on Day 3 before auto-accept.

### Packaging Tips to Reduce Weight Errors

- Package size should exactly fit the product.
- Measure dimensions of the **package**, not the product.
- Maintain consistent packaging across similar products.
- Ensure packaging weight is proportional to product weight.
- Photograph packages from multiple angles (length, width, height) and on a digital scale to document weight clearly.

### Couriers with Image-Based Weight Dispute

Sellers can map SKUs to saved packages and request volumetric weight freezing by providing image proofs — this can prevent weight discrepancy charges for shipments using those packages.

**Path:** Setup and Manage > Packaging > Package Master > Upload Images

## How to Do on Seller Panel

### View Weight Discrepancies

1. Go to **Billing > Weight Discrepancy** (or via the Weight panel from the left menu).
2. View the discrepancy details: date, AWB, product info, applied weight vs. entered weight.

### Accept a Weight Discrepancy

1. Go to **Billing > Weight Discrepancy**.
2. Find the discrepancy and click **Accept**.
3. The on-hold amount is charged.

### Raise a Weight Dispute

1. Go to **Billing > Weight Discrepancy**.
2. Find the discrepancy and click **Dispute**.
3. Upload images and evidence if available (e.g., package photos with dimensions, weight scale).
4. Submit the dispute.

> Disputes must be raised within **7 days** to avoid auto-accept.

### Save Package Dimensions to Avoid Errors

Go to **Setup and Manage > Packaging > Package Master** > Save packages with correct dimensions. Map SKUs to packages and upload image proofs to request volumetric weight freezing.

## Mobile App — How to Do

### View Weight Discrepancies (Mobile)

1. Tap three-dots menu at bottom.
2. Tap **Finance** or **Weight Discrepancy** (depending on app version).
3. View new discrepancies and take action.

### Act on a Weight Discrepancy (Mobile)

1. Find the discrepancy.
2. Tap **Accept** to accept or **Dispute** to raise a dispute.
3. Upload supporting images if disputing.
