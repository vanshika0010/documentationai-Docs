## Product Info

### In-Transit Damage

A shipment may be scanned as "In-Transit Damage" when it is damaged during the courier's transit. Sellers must share pre-shipment (preship) images with a visible shipping label for investigation.

### Shipment Misroute

Misroute occurs when a shipment is sent to the wrong delivery location by the courier. A revised EDD is usually provided. If it is faster to reship than wait — seller can create a new order and request RTO on the misrouted shipment. Freight refund applies for both shipments.

## SOP — In-Transit Damage

### System Check

1. Copy link from SPARK and paste in a new tab / Click on Internal ICRM.
2. View Details > Shipment section.
3. Check shipment status.

### Scenario 1 — Seller has NOT shared preship images

> Sir / Ma'am, as I checked here, your shipment status is showing in-transit damage. I would like to request you to please share preship images with the shipping label. Our team will provide you a resolution within 24–48 hours. I am sharing an email on your registered email ID — kindly share the proof.

### Scenario 2 — Seller HAS already shared preship images

> Sir / Ma'am, as I checked here, your shipment status is showing in-transit damage and preship images have already been shared by you. I request you to please wait 24–48 hours for resolution.

> **Note:** If seller is unable to share images from their end, or asks about the image for damage — raise an Open ticket.

## SOP — Shipment Misroute

### System Check

1. Click on the link from SPARK for AWB details.
2. Confirm shipment details > Check status > View Details > Shipment tab > Check EDD.
3. Check SR Tracking.

### Resolution Steps

1. Check revised EDD on ICRM and inform seller.
2. If it is faster to ship a new order than wait for delivery of the misrouted shipment — ask seller to ship another order and RTO the misrouted one.
3. Provide freight refund for both shipments.
4. Raise LOPS ticket for RTO.
5. Fill the [Misrouted Shipment Tracker](https://docs.google.com/forms/d/e/1FAIpQLSe_aw0Ugyd-FwzNSSLbNTS5G5zRU-Qz83vKed44_F15CRG77w/viewform?pli=1) — BPOC team will process refund the same day.

## How to Do on Seller Panel

### Check In-Transit Damaged Orders

1. Go to **Orders > In-Transit tab**.
2. Click **Select Status** dropdown.
3. Select **Damaged**.
4. View all damaged shipments.

### Raise a Dispute for Damaged Shipment

1. Go to **Orders** from the left menu.
2. Search for the AWB.
3. Click **More (...) > Create Ticket**.
4. Select **Wrong/Damaged/Partial/Empty Package Delivered**.
5. Upload preship images (with shipping label visible) and any other proof.
6. Click **Submit**.

> Disputes must be raised **within 72 hours** of delivery for delivered shipments. For in-transit damage, contact support as soon as the status is visible.

### Track a Misrouted Shipment

1. Go to **Orders** from the left menu.
2. Search by AWB number.
3. Check the tracking remarks for the updated location and EDD.

### Request Freight Refund (Misroute)

Freight refund for misrouted shipments is processed via the internal Misrouted Shipment Tracker — fill it and the BPOC team processes the refund the same day.

## Mobile App — How to Do

### Check Damaged Orders (Mobile)

1. From the bottom navigation, tap **Orders**.
2. Go to **In-Transit** tab.
3. Use the **Select Status** filter > select **Damaged**.

### Report In-Transit Damage (Mobile)

1. Find the damaged order.
2. Tap **Create Ticket** or **More options**.
3. Select the appropriate dispute type.
4. Upload images (preship images with AWB label visible).
5. Submit the ticket.
